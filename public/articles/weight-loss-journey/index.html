My weight loss journey

Insights into my weight loss journey, health and diet
HealthWeight-Loss 3 min

I started my weight loss journey, with hit and misses in 2021 when the scale indicated ~100kg. I’ve dropped weight, gained again and I’m now back on track with losing weight.

I achieved this result by:

  • Applying intermittent fasting (8 hour feeding window)
  • Keto-style diet
  • PronoKal

I also experimented with 48 and and even a +70 hour fasting window to provoke cell regeneration. Eventually my progress halted, and I needed to take a closer look at my daily caloric intake because simply put: more calories in than burned/consumed = fat storage

I made it a habit to keep track on my food intake (using YAZIO). Periods of weight loss directly correlate with my adherence to this practice of monitoring my intake and calories burned.

Even though I kept bouncing back up once I reached my target weight of 75kg I’m still very satisfied I managed to keep the weight off so far. According to Layne Norton, in his book “Fat loss Forever”1, only 5% of people who undertake a weight loss journey actually manage to keep the weight off.

The main thing for me is weighing myself weekly. I switched from periods where I strictly counted calories and weighing my food to eyeballing things and trying to eat intuitively, which almost always resulted in weight gain but as soon as I started creeping back to the 80 kg mark, I was at least aware and switched back to a more rigorous routine.

Goal

Allthough I’m happy keeping the weight off, I’m not satisfied with my current weight as I have a second goal of reaching 10% body fat. I realized that simply losing weight was not going to cut it anymore. I needed to become more intentional with my weight loss, more mindful.

I did some research on goal setting and realized I needed a clear target within a specific time frame, just like you need SMART objectives at work.

However again my weight started to creep up from 76kg to 80kg. Since my breaking point seemed to revolve around 76kg, I set a new target of 75kg within a 2 month time frame. My starting weight was 78kg and my most recent weigh-in indicated 77.2kg. So far it’s working, losing around 0,5kg of weight a week, which is a healthy amount you can realistically lose.

I made a little sheet of paper and it’s hanging on my fridge. Every day I mark with an X whether I adhered to my target of 1700kcal +/- 100kcal. Squares indicate a day where I do a 500kcal fasting day.

Weight loss interventions

  • Calorie daily target: 1700kcal
  • Two meals a day in a four hour eating window
  • 20 hours of fasting a day
  • Tracking all calories, even when overeating
  • 5:2 pattern week: two days of fasting a week where I eat a small meal of max. 500 kcal
  • Running 3+ times a week
  • Playing padel 2+ times a week

Listed out like this, it seems extreme to lose only a few kilograms of fat but apparently it’s necessary for me. When I start losing more that 0,5kg/week, I know I need to adjust my scheme by either increasing my caloric intake, or lower my activity levels.

Accountability

When I made this plan, I shared it with my wife and a few friends, including the post(s) on my website. I’ve been on this journey alone but since I seem to get stuck at 76kg and rebounce, I upped the ante and involved others. It helps to stick with the program as it would be embarassing to make this kind of bold committment and not sticking with it.
