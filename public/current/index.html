Weight loss

I’ve been on and off a weight loss regime for the past ten years with some successes and failures. Once the scale indicated +100kg I knew I had to do something about it. I decided to try a keto diet. It worked only to a certain extend for me (lost about 10kg).

After I stopped eating keto-style, I immediately gained weight again. I decided to try Pronokal. During this period I lost 25kg in only three months, which is also the problem. I consider it a crash diet with support from a doctor. But once you stop eating their way, just like keto, I gained again the same amount of weight in roughly the same amount of time.

Fast-forward to the present day and I was once again on my way to ~100kg. I shifted my regular diet.

You can read more about my progress on my weight loss journey.

Diet

  • eating yoghurt and fruit as breakfast
  • some form of toast (crackers) with chicken or turkey
  • regular dinner while watching my total daily carb. & calories intake

I quit snacking and candy and must say this sort of lifestyle is maintainable for a longer period. I’m not losing weight as fast as before (still managed to go down 6kg in a month) but feel this is much more sustainable. I have cheat meal once a week.

Fasting

I tried OMAD (one meal a day) and intermittent fasting but for some reason it wasn’t working for me. Probably as my total caloric intake at that time was still too high for the low level of activity I had in a day. However talking to many dietists they almost all recommended to first get three meals a day again, to increase my metabolism.

Habits

Morning light

I try to get at least ten minutes of direct sunlight within two hours after waking up.

Exercise

Playing padel 2-5x a week also aids in being in a caloric deficit, slowly but steadily losing weight.

My current goal for 2024 is to get to a rhythm where I have 30 minutes of exercise each day. I’ll try to pick up running as well. Currently on and off depending on how I feel and the majority of runs are (late) evening. A goal this year is to get up earlier, so I can get a run in before work, on an empty stomach.

I currently don’t perform any kind of weight exercises, not even body weight. I might try to incorporate a small body weight routine in the near future.

Yoga, such as Ashtanga session, I do to focus on stretching my back (scoliosis) and hamstrings. I do these in the afternoon when I’m working from home.

After dinner I try to go for a brisk walk of twenty minutes.

Meditation

I practice formal sitting meditation for 30-60 minutes a day. Throughout my daily activities I strive to maintain continuous mindfulness. Running itself has also become sort of a meditation and I try to get at least three sessions in a week, of 45 minutes or more.

Cleaning

I keep my working environment clean and clutter free. It aids in mental clarity, focus and well being. I strive to keep our house and my home office in particular to always be presentable to visitors at any time of the day.

Work

I work as a scrum master & agile coach for a large outdoor gear retailer in Europe. I’m a certified SAFe agilist and daily aid our organisation with its transition to agile.

Health

Sleep

My aim is to get a solid 7 hours of sleep a day. My six month average is around 6 hours, so improving the amount (and quality) of sleep I get is another goal for 2024.

Sobriety

I never did any drugs, except for alcohol. In the past I drank at least a few times a week. Right now I’m not completely sober, but I only drink very occassionally and limit myself to one or two glasses of beer, wine, etc. Cutting out alcohol also aids my weight loss journey as I cut carbs and sugars. As a side note - after a few weeks, or months, you don’t miss it and the alternatives like 0% beer also tastes a lot better if you don’t have an accurate memory of how regular beer used to taste.

Spirituality

I’m practicing Buddhism. I found my religion in Therevadan Buddhism and I found that the Thai Forest Tradition of Ajahn Chah suits me best. I’ve been on the five precepts for nearly five years now.
